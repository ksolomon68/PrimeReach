Small Business Eligibility

Learn about Small Business certification requirements and benefits

What is a Small Business?

A Small Business is a business that meets specific size and ownership criteria. Small Business certification provides access to set-aside contracting opportunities and support services designed to help small businesses grow and succeed.

What PrimeReach does: This portal helps you prepare for contracting opportunities — through training, bid readiness support, and resource navigation. You do not need to be certified to use this platform.
Certification authority and formal contracting rules are governed separately. Official Small Business certification is issued through the California Office of the Small Business Advocate (CalOSBA) (opens in new window)↗. DBE certification is administered through California UCP (opens in new window)↗. BizConnect does not certify businesses and is not where official bids are awarded.

Eligibility Requirements

1. Business Size

Your business must meet the Small Business Administration (SBA) size standards for your industry. Size standards are typically based on:

  • Number of employees, or
  • Average annual receipts

2. Ownership

The business must be:

  • At least 51% owned by one or more individuals who are U.S. citizens or lawful permanent residents
  • Independently owned and operated
  • Not dominant in its field of operation

3. Control

The qualifying owner(s) must:

  • Control the management and daily operations
  • Make independent business decisions
  • Possess the expertise and technical competence to perform the work

4. Principal Place of Business

Your business must have its principal place of business in California or perform a commercially useful function on your agency contracts.

Required Documentation

To apply for Small Business certification, you'll typically need to provide:

  • Business license and formation documents
  • Federal Tax ID (EIN) documentation
  • Personal financial statements of owners
  • Business tax returns (typically last 3 years)
  • Organizational documents (articles of incorporation, bylaws, operating agreement)
  • Stock certificates or ownership agreements
  • Resumes of key personnel
  • Contractor's license (if applicable)

Application Process

1

Review Requirements

Ensure your business meets all eligibility criteria and gather required documentation.

2

Complete Application

Fill out the Small Business certification application with detailed information about your business ownership, operations, and finances.

3

Submit Documentation

Upload all required supporting documents with your application.

4

Application Review

Your agency will review your application and may request additional information or clarification.

5

Receive Decision

You'll receive notification of the certification decision, typically within 90 days of submitting a complete application.

Benefits of Small Business Certification

  • Access to Set-Aside Contracts: Compete for contracts specifically designated for small businesses
  • Increased Visibility: Be listed in the Your Agency Small Business directory
  • Networking Opportunities: Connect with prime contractors and other agencies
  • Technical Assistance: Access to business development and training programs
  • Competitive Advantage: Many opportunities prefer or require Small Business certification

Need Help with Certification?

Our support services team can help you understand the requirements and guide you through the application process.
